K-Drama History

The evolution of Korean television drama

1960s - The Beginning

The first Korean television dramas aired, marking the birth of what would become a global phenomenon. Simple stories with powerful emotions.

1990s - The Golden Age

K-dramas gained popularity across Asia with romantic comedies and family dramas. This era established many classic storytelling formulas.

2000s - Hallyu Wave

The Korean Wave (Hallyu) began spreading Korean culture globally. Dramas like "Winter Sonata" captivated international audiences.

2010s - Global Recognition

High production values and diverse genres attracted worldwide attention. Streaming platforms made K-dramas accessible globally.

2020s - Mainstream Success

K-dramas became mainstream entertainment worldwide. "Squid Game" broke Netflix records and won multiple international awards.
